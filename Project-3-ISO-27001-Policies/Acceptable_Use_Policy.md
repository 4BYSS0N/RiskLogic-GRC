## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to outline the acceptable use of information systems at FinTech-Scale India. These rules are established to protect both the individual user and the organization, ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of corporate assets.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, and third-party agents operating on behalf of FinTech-Scale India. It covers all information technology equipment, software, and data owned or leased by the organization.

---

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Device Security
* Users are responsible for the physical and logical security of company-issued devices.
* **Clean Desk Policy:** Users must ensure that workstations are locked whenever they are left unattended. 
* Mobile devices and laptops must not be left in unsecured environments.

### 3.2 Password Integrity
* Users must adhere to defined password complexity requirements (minimum 12 characters, including alphanumeric and special characters).
* Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) must be enabled on all corporate accounts where supported.
* The sharing of credentials, passwords, or session tokens is strictly prohibited.

### 3.3 Prohibited Activities
* Use of company systems for illegal activities, harassment, or the distribution of defamatory material is prohibited.
* **Shadow IT:** The installation of unauthorized software or the use of unapproved third-party cloud services for corporate data is a violation of this policy.
* Users shall not attempt to circumvent or disable security controls.

### 3.4 Data Handling
* Sensitive customer data (PII) must never be stored on personal devices or unencrypted removable media.
* All data handling must align with the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act 2023 requirements for "reasonable security safeguards."

---

## 4. Reporting
Users are obligated to report any suspected security weaknesses, lost devices, or potential security incidents to the Information Security Team immediately through the established reporting channels.

---

## 5. Enforcement
Any individual found to have violated this policy may be subject to disciplinary action. This may include, but is not limited to, the loss of system access, formal warnings, or termination of employment/contract, in accordance with applicable local laws and organizational regulations.
